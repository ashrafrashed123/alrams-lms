📖 Reading 1: Chinese New Year

6 Questions × 4 Marks = 24 Marks

Chinese New Year is an important festival celebrated in China and many other countries. The festival usually takes place between January and February. Families clean and decorate their homes with red colours because red is believed to bring good luck.

People often visit relatives, exchange gifts, and enjoy large family meals together. Children sometimes receive money in red envelopes from older family members. Fireworks and dragon dances are also popular during the celebrations.

Each year in the Chinese calendar is connected to a different animal, such as the tiger, rabbit, or dragon. Many people believe these animals can influence personality and luck. The festival is a time for families to come together and welcome the new year happily.

One important part of the festival is the camel beauty competition. Camel owners bring their camels from many regions of the UAE and nearby countries. Kodomo no Hi: Children’s Day in Japan

May 5th is an important national holiday in Japan called Kodomo no Hi, or Children's Day. The celebration focuses on respecting children, supporting their development, and recognising their happiness. Although the festival was originally connected mainly with boys, modern celebrations include all children and families. It is a national holiday to respect children's personalities and to celebrate their happiness. Originally, this day was only for boys, but now it is for all children.

On this day, families fly large cloth flags called "koinobori". These flags look like carp fish and blow in the wind. The black flag represents the father, the red flag represents the mother, and the smaller flags represent the children.

Children also eat special traditional sweets on this day. It is a time for families to say thank you to their mothers and fathers. Schools often have sports events or special activities for the students.
