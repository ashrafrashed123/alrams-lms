Section 3 — Reading Passage 1: National Festivals
📌 6 Questions  |  4 marks each  |  Total: 24 marks  |  Exercise: True / False / Not Given

National festivals are celebrated all over the world to bring communities together and honor cultural heritage. In many countries, these celebrations are massive events that require months of preparation. People decorate streets with colorful banners, bright lights, and traditional lanterns. Families gather to share delicious meals, exchange traditional sweets, and enjoy music.

One major aspect of these national celebrations is the parade. Large crowds gather along the main roads to watch dancers, musicians, and beautiful floats pass by. For many citizens, participating in or watching these events is an important experience that strengthens their sense of belonging to their country. In the United Arab Emirates, National Day is a prime example, where people wave flags and express pride in their history.

While some festivals are religious, national festivals focus primarily on the history, achievements, and unity of a specific country. They provide a wonderful opportunity for visitors to learn about local traditions, clothes, and traditional food. Many schools and businesses close for the day so that everyone can fully participate. These events are essential for keeping ancient cultural practices alive for younger generations who might otherwise forget them in the modern world.

My African Adventure Blog  — Posted by Tariq

Last month, I finally fulfilled my lifelong dream of exploring the incredible landscapes of East Africa. My journey began in Tanzania, where our expedition group set out early in the morning to explore the famous Serengeti. Traveling across the wide grasslands in an open-top vehicle was an experience I will never forget. The terrain was beautiful but dangerous, filled with wild predators hunting for their prey.

Our local guide, Juma, was an expert. He could spot a leopard hidden in a tree from a long distance. On our third day, we faced a terrible situation when our vehicle got stuck in deep mud near a river. It was a potential disaster because the sun was setting, and wild animals were active. Luckily, Juma used a strong rope and a nearby tree to pull the vehicle out safely. No one got hurt, but it was a scary reminder of how unpredictable nature can be.

Later, we visited a traditional village. The local people welcomed our crowd of travelers with bright smiles and delicious food. They performed a traditional dance around a campfire under the night sky. This trip was expensive, but the memories of the wildlife and the wonderful people I met made every single dirham worth it.
