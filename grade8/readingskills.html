Reading 1: An Invitation I Did Not Expect

14 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 2 marks each

Last Thursday, I received an email from my friend Samir. He invited me to join him at the International Food Festival in our city. At first, I was not sure if I should go because I had planned to stay home and finish my homework. However, the email sounded exciting. Samir wrote that the festival would take place at City Park on Saturday from 3 p.m. to 9 p.m. He also said that people could try food from many countries, including Emirati, Indian, Italian and Japanese dishes.

On Saturday afternoon, I met Samir at the entrance. The place was crowded, but it looked colourful and cheerful. There were food stalls, music and families everywhere. First, we tried Emirati food because Samir said it was his favourite. Then we tasted some spicy Indian snacks. I thought they would be too hot, but they were actually delicious.

Later, we listened to a group of children singing on a small stage. After that, we joined a short cooking activity. A chef showed us how to make a simple salad. Before we left, Samir bought some sweets for his little sister.

When I got home, I realised I had learned something important. Trying new things with a friend can turn an ordinary day into a special memory.

Reading 2: The Festival in the School Yard

14 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 2 marks each

Last month, our school organised a small children's festival in the school yard. The event was planned for younger students, but older students were invited to help. I volunteered because I enjoy working with children, and I wanted to spend the day doing something useful.

We arrived early in the morning to decorate the place. Some students hung colourful paper flags, while others arranged chairs near the stage. My job was to help at the games corner. At first, I thought the children would be shy, but they were full of energy. They ran from one activity to another and laughed loudly all morning.

At ten o'clock, the festival officially started. There were races, face painting and short performances. One of the most popular activities was a fishing game made from paper fish and small sticks. Many children stood in line because they wanted to win small prizes. Later, the head teacher gave a short speech about how festivals bring people together.

In the afternoon, we shared juice and sandwiches. By the end of the day, I was very tired, but I felt proud. I understood that a successful festival is not only about fun. It is also about teamwork, kindness and creating happy moments for others.

Reading 3: A Message from My Cousin

14 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 2 marks each

Two days ago, my cousin Huda sent me a message asking if I would like to go to a festival with her. She wrote that a family festival was taking place near the Corniche and that there would be music, games and food stalls. I replied quickly because I did not want to miss the chance. We agreed to meet at four o'clock near the main gate.

When I arrived, Huda was already there with her younger brother. The festival looked lively. Bright banners hung above the entrance, and children were carrying balloons. First, we walked around to see everything. Then Huda suggested that we watch a puppet show. Her brother laughed so much that even the people around us started smiling.

After the show, we bought corn and juice. We sat on a bench and watched families moving from one activity to another. Later, we visited a craft tent where children were making masks. Huda's brother wanted to stay longer, but it was getting late. Before leaving, we took a photo together in front of a large sign that said "Family Festival Day".

On the way home, I thought about how simple invitations can lead to wonderful experiences. If Huda had not sent that message, I would have spent the evening at home doing nothing special.

Reading 4: Our Trip to Children's Day

14 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 2 marks each

Last year, my family and I visited a Children's Day festival at a large community centre. My little brother was especially excited because he had heard there would be games, songs and gifts. We arrived just after lunch, and the festival had already begun. Loud music came from the stage, and volunteers welcomed families at the entrance.

At first, my brother wanted to try every activity at once. He ran to the art table, then to the jumping area, and then to a storytelling corner. My parents laughed and told him to slow down. I decided to stay with him while my parents looked for a place to sit. Soon, we joined a drawing competition. The children were asked to draw their happiest day. My brother drew our family at the beach, and I drew a picnic in the park.

Later, a woman on the stage announced the winners of different games. My brother did not win a prize, but he did not seem disappointed. He said the best part was meeting other children and playing with them. Before we left, each child received a small gift bag with a pencil, sweets and a toy.

That evening, I realised children do not always need expensive things to be happy. Sometimes, they only need time, attention and a place to have fun together.

Reading 5: The Best Stall at the Festival

14 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 2 marks each

On Friday evening, my friends and I went to a food festival near our neighbourhood. We had heard about it from a poster at school. The poster said there would be food from many countries and activities for families. Since we all enjoy trying unusual dishes, we decided to go together after sunset.

As soon as we entered, we noticed the smell of grilled food in the air. There were long rows of stalls with bright signs above them. My friend Kareem wanted to try everything, but I suggested that we walk around first and choose carefully. That was a good idea because some stalls sold meals, while others sold desserts or drinks.

After walking for ten minutes, we stopped at a stall that served Moroccan food. The cook was friendly and explained the dishes to us. I chose a chicken meal with rice, and Kareem picked a spicy soup. We both liked our food, but the best part of the evening came later when we found a dessert stall selling sweet pastries with honey.

Before leaving, we voted on our favourite stall. Most of us chose the dessert stall, not only because the food was excellent, but also because the seller was cheerful and generous. It showed me that good service can make food taste even better.
