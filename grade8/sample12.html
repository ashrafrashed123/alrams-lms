Part 3A: Reading – UAE National Day (24 Marks)

Towards Grade Level Goal  |  Expository text (A2.1–A2.2)

Read the text. Choose the correct answer. Choose True, False or Not Given for each statement.

UAE National Day is celebrated on the second of December every year. It marks the historic moment in 1971 when the seven emirates officially came together to form the United Arab Emirates. The national holiday lasts for two days, and people from all across the country take part in the celebrations.

During UAE National Day, cities and towns are beautifully decorated with the national flag and lights in the colours red, green, white, and black. Many people wear traditional Emirati clothing and paint their faces with the national colours to show their pride. Spectacular fireworks fill the sky at night, and large concerts and cultural performances are organised in public spaces throughout the country.

One of the most popular traditions during the celebrations is the car parade. People decorate their cars with flags, lights, and patriotic symbols and drive proudly through the streets. Families often gather together for large meals and watch the celebrations on television if they are unable to attend in person.

Schools across the UAE prepare for National Day several weeks before the holiday. Students take part in art competitions, poetry readings, and cultural performances. Teachers often ask students to research the history of the UAE so that they can understand how the country developed. National Day is a time for all Emiratis and residents to feel proud of their nation and celebrate its achievements together.

Part 3B: Reading – My Adventure in Kenya (28 Marks)

At Grade Level Goal  |  Narrative text – Blog Post (A2.2–B1.1)

Read the text and answer the questions. Choose A, B or C.

My Adventure in Kenya  |  By Sam Walker  |  Travel & Exploration Blog
I have always dreamed of visiting Africa, and last month I finally made that dream come true. I flew to Nairobi, the capital city of Kenya, and spent three weeks exploring this extraordinary country.

My favourite experience was visiting the Maasai Mara National Reserve. I arrived there early in the morning, just as the sun was slowly rising over the golden plains. As I was sitting quietly in the jeep, I suddenly spotted a group of lions walking calmly through the long grass. I could not believe how close they were to us. My heart was beating so fast that I could hardly breathe!

I also had the wonderful chance to meet a local Maasai community. The Maasai people have lived in this part of Africa for hundreds of years, and they were incredibly warm and welcoming. They performed their traditional dances and shared fascinating stories about their way of life. I was deeply moved by their kindness and hospitality.

Travelling in Kenya was far more exciting than I had ever imagined. The landscapes were breathtaking, the wildlife was unforgettable, and the people were truly wonderful. I am already planning my next trip back to this amazing continent.

Have you ever visited Africa? I would love to hear about your own travel adventures in the comments below!

I hope you're doing well! I'm writing because I've just found out about an amazing event that I think you'll absolutely love. Next Saturday, there's going to be an International Food Festival in Al Ain City Park, and I really think we should go together.

The festival celebrates food traditions from over thirty different countries. There will be stalls offering everything from Japanese sushi and Indian curries to Mexican tacos and French pastries. What makes it truly special is that many of the chefs are actually from those countries themselves, so the food is completely authentic – meaning it tastes exactly as it would in its home country.

As well as eating, there will be cooking demonstrations where professional chefs show the audience how to prepare traditional dishes step by step. I have always wanted to learn how to make fresh pasta from scratch, so I am definitely planning to attend that session!

The festival runs from ten in the morning until ten at night. Entry is free for children under twelve, and adult tickets cost fifty dirhams. I think that is excellent value considering everything that is included.

I am meeting my cousin there at midday. Would you like to join us? Please let me know as soon as possible so that I can book the tickets in time.

Looking forward to hearing from you!
