Ocean Exploration is becoming more popular as technology improves. In the past, only trained astronauts could travel beyond Earth. Today, several private companies are developing spacecraft that may allow ordinary people to experience space travel.

Although tickets are currently very expensive, supporters believe that prices may decrease in the future. Companies are testing new vehicles designed to carry small groups of passengers on short journeys above Earth. During these trips, travellers can experience weightlessness and enjoy breathtaking views of the planet.

However, some people are concerned about the environmental impact of frequent launches. Others question whether space tourism should be a priority while there are still many problems to solve on Earth. Some experts also worry that only wealthy people will be able to afford such journeys.

Despite these concerns, interest in space tourism continues to grow. Many experts believe that technological advances made for tourism could also support scientific research and future exploration.

Part 3B: Reading

28 marks · Multiple Choice

Diwali Festival is celebrated in several Asian countries during the autumn season. It is one of the most important cultural events of the year and is known for its colourful traditions and family gatherings. During the festival, people often admire the full moon, which symbolises unity and togetherness.

One of the most popular traditions is eating mooncakes. These special pastries come in many flavours and are often shared among family members and friends. Children also carry lanterns through parks and streets, creating a festive atmosphere.

In modern cities, public celebrations may include music, performances, and cultural exhibitions. Although some customs have changed over time, many families continue to follow traditions that have been passed down for generations.

The festival remains important because it strengthens family relationships and helps preserve cultural heritage.

Part 3C: Reading

28 marks · Multiple Choice

Mars is one of the most studied planets in our solar system. Scientists are interested in Mars because it may provide clues about the history of planets and the possibility of life beyond Earth.

Over the years, robotic missions have collected information about the planet's surface, atmosphere, and climate. Rovers have travelled across rocky landscapes, sending images and scientific data back to Earth. These discoveries have helped researchers understand how Mars has changed over time.

Future missions may include sending astronauts to Mars. However, such journeys present many challenges. Travellers would need food, water, and protection from harmful radiation during the long trip. Engineers must also design systems that can support life far from Earth.

Despite these difficulties, many scientists believe that exploring Mars could lead to important discoveries and inspire future generations.
