Part 3A: Reading

24 marks · True / False / Not Given

Every year, millions of Muslims travel to Makkah to perform Hajj. This important journey brings together people from many countries and cultures. Although pilgrims speak different languages and come from different backgrounds, they all share the same purpose.

During Hajj, pilgrims complete a series of religious activities over several days. They pray, visit holy places, and spend time reflecting on their lives. Because such a large number of people gather in one area, careful organisation is necessary. Authorities provide transportation, medical support, and guidance to help everyone stay safe.

Many pilgrims describe Hajj as one of the most meaningful experiences of their lives. They often return home with a stronger sense of gratitude and community. Modern technology has improved the journey by providing maps, updates, and useful information through mobile applications.

Although the journey can be physically demanding, most pilgrims believe that the spiritual rewards make the effort worthwhile.

Part 3B: Reading

28 marks · Multiple Choice

The deep ocean is one of the least explored places on Earth. Although scientists have studied the sea for many years, large areas remain unknown. Conditions in the deep ocean are difficult because sunlight cannot reach these depths, and the water pressure is extremely high.

To study these environments, researchers use special submarines and robotic vehicles. These machines can travel to places that would be too dangerous for humans. Scientists have discovered unusual creatures that survive without sunlight. Some depend on chemicals released from the ocean floor instead of plants for energy.

Ocean exploration is important because it helps scientists understand ecosystems and may lead to discoveries in medicine and technology. However, experts also stress the importance of protecting delicate underwater habitats while conducting research. If these habitats are damaged, some species could disappear before scientists fully understand them.

Part 3C: Reading

28 marks · Multiple Choice

The International Space Station travels around Earth at a very high speed. Astronauts from different countries live and work there for several months at a time. Their duties include conducting experiments, repairing equipment, and observing Earth from space.

Life on the space station differs from life on Earth because of microgravity. Objects float, and astronauts must learn new ways to perform everyday tasks. Activities such as eating, sleeping, and exercising require special techniques and careful planning.

Scientists value the station because it allows them to study how people and materials behave in space. The information collected there may help future missions travel farther into the solar system.

Although living in space can be challenging, many astronauts describe the experience as rewarding. They often say that seeing Earth from above changes the way they think about the planet and its environment.
