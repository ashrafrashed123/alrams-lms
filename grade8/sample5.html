Part 3A: Reading – Towards GLG

The Janadriyah National Festival is a very important cultural event in Saudi Arabia. It first started in the year 1985 and it celebrates the rich traditions and history of the country. The festival usually takes place in February or March and lasts for two whole weeks. Millions of people travel from all over Saudi Arabia and from other countries to enjoy the wonderful celebration with their families.

The festival always starts with a huge, exciting camel race, which is very popular with visitors of all ages. There are also horse races, falconry shows, and traditional poetry readings. For people who love shopping, there is an enormous market at the festival. At this market, you can buy beautiful traditional crafts, colourful clothes, and expensive jewellery. Visitors can also try many delicious traditional foods from different parts of the country. A large book fair is also part of the festival, where people can find many interesting books to read.

Similar to the Janadriyah Festival, the UAE also has an important national celebration. UAE National Day is celebrated every year on the 2nd of December. It marks the day when the emirates came together to form one country in 1971. Families decorate their homes and watch magnificent fireworks.

Part 3B: Reading – At GLG

28 marks · Multiple Choice (A, B, C) · 4 marks each

My Trip to Gabon

I will never forget my incredible journey to the west coast of Africa. I arrived in the country of Gabon in 1895. I am a European explorer, and my plan was to travel up the famous Ogoouè River. I wanted to study the way people lived in that part of the world, and I also wanted to explore the deep river.

On the 5th of June, I finally left the port of Glass. At first, I travelled by a large boat to a place called Ndjolè. It was a long trip, and the weather was very hot. When I was travelling, I had to change to a small canoe because the big boats couldn't travel further up the river. The water was getting too shallow and narrow for a large ship.

As I was going up the river in my canoe, I stopped many times to collect fish. The river was full of life! I collected 65 different types of fish during my journey. Surprisingly, three of these fish were later named after me because they were completely new discoveries. It was dangerous sometimes, but I loved every minute of it. Two years later, in 1897, I wrote a book about my amazing travels in Africa.
